Aces Tracking System©

USER MANUAL

Thank you for subscribing to Kids at Hope’s Aces Tracking System. Kids at Hope believes that the Aces Tracking System will truly transform the understanding about what a meaningful and sustainable relationship between adult and child is and how we monitor, validate and document that information in support of all children’s futures, without exception.

GUIDELINES FOR USE

The Aces Tracking exercise should be utilized a minimum of twice a school or program year. The first time should be no earlier than 8 weeks into the year so that adults have had a chance to form the meaningful relationships that the Aces Tracking System will trace. Once all adults have entered the aces they give to children, you will be able to view your results in a Total Aces Report. From this report you will be able to identify which children you serve have aces and those who have no aces.

Universal Truth #2: Children succeed when they have meaningful and sustainable relationships with caring adults.

Children with no aces have been identified as not having a single, significant relationship with any adults in your organization. And the research tells us those children who have meaningful, sustainable relationships with caring adults have a much higher chance of success than children who grow up disconnected and anonymous. Once the children with no aces or very few aces have been identified, it is imperative to work on connecting those children with adults who care about them.

The Aces Tracking System can also be used to identify the adults who connect to specific children should a child need a caring adult at any time they are in your care. Viewing an Individual Student Report will list out for you every adult in your organization who has identified themselves as having a meaningful relationship with that child. This resource can prove to be enormously important when working with children on an individual basis.
